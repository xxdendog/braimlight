Light for outdoors: Creating attractive and safely places at night

In addition to the need for safety and security, night-time illumination also fulfils cultural and commercial purposes. Light promotes public life at night.

001 / 004

Illuminating trees

Convey a sense of security

Illuminated trees form visual axes in the dark and convey a sense of security. We offer helpful tips for the lighting design, and show you how to impressively illuminate various types of trees and palms.

002 / 004

illuminating facades

Make buildings visible

Impressive facade lighting makes buildings visible in the urban space and enables orientation. We give you tips on how to strikingly illuminate facades in unison with the needs of both people and the environment.

003 / 004

illuminating paths

Provide orientation

Paths connect places. When dusk falls, light without glare makes paths safe and provides orientation. Successful path lighting is characterised by the fact that it fulfils design tasks as well as functional needs.

004 / 004

art in outdoor spaces

Unique visitor experiences

Be it a museum sculpture garden, an exhibit in a private area outdoors or an artwork displayed in a public park: bring outdoor art to life at night and create unique visitor experiences. Each work of art is requires different light.

braim lighting use different layers of light to deliver the greatest effect

The type and sequence of lighting scenes are defined in a kind of script. We are appreciate a space in all three dimensions and use the right balance of shadow and light.

01 — 03

Architectural lighting

Architectural lighting emphasises the design of a building, improves the perception of urban objects and draws attention to decorative elements.

02 — 03

General lighting

General ambient lighting increases the feeling of safety. By contrast, light for orientation is directed at prominent objects, trees or vertical areas, helping to ensure safe navigation through the urban space.

03 — 03

Sensitive light

Light has a stimulating effect and supports positive emotions. It brings people together, encouraging them to stay a while in a certain place. Dynamic and interactive lighting scenes combine with works of light art to carry out these functions.

Multi-layered user scenarios: lighting design reflects the time and the location

Urban planners utilise the dynamic power of light. Multi-layered user scenarios play with changing brightness levels, colour temperatures and light atmospheres.

001 / 003

public and private parks

Late evening

The park is full of life. Trees lit vertically improve spatial perception. Softly accentuated buildings complete the nocturnal cityscape.

Middle of the night

A few people crossing the park, accompanied by adaptive light. Vertical illumination of the trees along the paths provides orientation and a feeling of safety.

Just before sunrise

High illuminance levels. Trees are vertically illuminated to show the way and gentle light on the pavements further enhances orientation.

002 / 003

Urban and public space

Late evening

People meet up and enjoy the city. The well-lit pavements and streets are still a hive of activity. The square and adjoining buildings are softly lit for general safety and orientation.

Middle of the night

The trees illuminated to help guide way for a few people. Prominent street furniture and vertical building surfaces are also gently lit to boost orientation.

Just before sunrise

High illuminance levels. Trees are vertically illuminated to show the way and gentle light on the pavements further enhances orientation.

003 / 003

Place for promenade

Late evening

The sun provides sufficient orientation. Connecting paths are additionally structured by lighting. The light colour adapts to the ambient conditions.

Middle of the night

Accent lighting of trees and buildings complements the functional illumination on pathways and streets. This allows better appreciation of the outdoor space.

Just before sunrise

Gentle illumination is enough for less frequented traffic routes when it is completely dark. Light accents on trees clearly show the way.
